Wyatt's Favorite Movies

Sunshine (2007), Directed by Danny Boyle, Starring Cillian Murphy, Rose Byrne, Cliff Curtis, Michelle Yeoh, Hiroyuki Sanada, Benedict Wong, Chris Evans, and Mark Strong.
A team of international astronauts is sent on a dangerous mission to reignite the dying Sun with a nuclear fission bomb in 2057.
Star Wars: Episode III - Revenge of the Sith (2005), Directed by George Lucas, Starring Ewen McGregor, Natalie Portman, Hayden Christensen, Ian McDiarmid, Samuel L. Jackson, Jimmy Smits, Frank Oz, and Anthony Daniels.
As the Clone Wars nears its end, Obi-Wan Kenobi pursues a new threat, while Anakin Skywalker is lured by Chancellor Palpatine into a sinister plot for galactic domination.
Jackie Brown (1997), Directed by Quinten Tarantino, Staring Pam Grier, Samuel L. Jackson, Robert Forester, Bridget Fonda, Michael Keaton, Robert De Niro, Michael Brown, and Chris Tucker.
A flight attendant with a criminal past gets nabbed by the ATF for smuggling. Under pressure to become an informant against the illegal arms dealer she works for, she must find a way to secure her future without getting killed.
Se7en (1995), Directed by David Fincher, Starring Morgan Freeman, Andrew Kevin Walker, Daniel Zacapa, Brad Pitt, Gwyneth Paltrow, John Cassini, Bob Mack, and Peter Crombie.
Two detectives, a rookie and a veteran, hunt a serial killer who uses the seven deadly sins as his motives.
Scream (1996), Directed by Wes Craven, Starring Drew Berrymore, Neve Campbell, Skeeet Ulrich, Courtney Cox, Rose McGowan, David Arquette, Matthew Lillard, and Jamie Kennedy.
In the small town of Woodsboro, California, a masked killer known as Ghostface begins murdering high school students, and a group of friends must use their knowledge of horror movies to unmask the killer.
New Nightmare (1994), Directed by Wes Craven, Starring Heather Langenkamp, Wes Craven, Robert Englund, JF Davis, Miko Hughes, Matt Winston, Marianne Maddalena, and Tracy Middendorf.
A demonic force has chosen Freddy Krueger as its portal to the real world. Can Heather Langenkamp play the part of Nancy one last time and trap the evil trying to enter our world?
The Prestige (2006), Directed by Christopher Nolan, Hugh Jackman, Christian Bale, Michael Caine, Piper Perabo, Rebecca Hall, Scarlett Johansson, Andy Serkis, amd David Bowie.
Rival 19th-century magicians engage in a bitter battle for trade secrets.
Alien (1979), Directed by Ridley Scott, Starring Tom Skerritt, Sigourney Weaver, Veronica Cartwright, Harry Dean Stanton, John Hurt, Ian Holm, Yaphet Kotto, and Bolaji Badejo as the Alien.
After investigating a mysterious transmission of unknown origin, the crew of a commercial spacecraft encounters a deadly lifeform.
The Big Lebowski (1998), Directed by Joel Coen, Starring Jeff Bridges, John Goodman, Julianne Moore, Steve Buscemi, Philip Seymour Hoffman, Tara Reid, and John Turturro.
Jeff "The Dude" Lebowski, mistaken for a millionaire of the same name, seeks restitution for his ruined rug and enlists his bowling buddies to help get it.
The Wolf of Wall Street (2013), Directed by Martin Scorsese, Starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Jonah Hill, Margot Robbie, Matthew McConaughey, Kyle Chandler, Rob Reiner, and Jon Bernthal.
Based on the true story of Jordan Belfort, from his rise to a wealthy stock-broker living the high life to his fall involving crime, corruption and the federal government.
The Truman Show (1998), Directed by Peter Weir, Starring Jim Carrey, Laura Linney, Ed Harris, Noah Emmerich, Natascha McElhone, Holland Taylor, and Paul Giamatti.
An insurance salesman begins to suspect that his whole life is actually some sort of reality TV show.
Big Fat Liar (2002), Directed by Shawn Levy, Starring Frankie Muniz, Amanda Bynes, Paul Giamatti, Amanda Detmer, Donald Faison, and Lee Majors.
Fourteen-year-old Jason Shepherd has a reputation for stretching the truth. So, when big-time Hollywood producer Marty Wolf steals his class paper and turns it into a smash hit movie, no one believes Jason's latest tall tale.
Rat Race (2001), Directed by Jerry Zucker, Starring Rowan Atkinson, Whoopi Goldberg, John Cleese, Cuba Gooding Jr., Seth Green, Jon Lovitz, Kathy Najimy, and Breckin Meyer.
A Las Vegas casino magnate determined to find a new avenue for wagering sets up a race for money.
The House That Jack Built (2018), Directed by Lars von Trier, Starring Matt Dillon, Bruno Ganz, Uma Thurman, Siobhan Fallon Hogan, Sofie Grabol, Riley Keough, and Jeremy Davies.
In five episodes, failed architect and vicious sociopath Jack recounts his elaborately orchestrated murders -- each, as he views them, a towering work of art that defines his life's work as a serial killer in the Pacific Northwest.
Madhouse (1990), Directed by Tom Ropelewski, Starring John Larroquette, Kirstie Alley, Alison La Placa, John Diehl, Jessica Lundy, Bradley Gregg, and Dennis Miller.
The luxurious villa of yuppie couple Mark and Jessie Bannister is overrun by loads of uninvited guests who turn the house upside-down and nearly destroy it.
These movies have several commonalities. They each come from specific, legendary directors each with their own distinct visions. Each is obsessed with quality and identity. Even with the funny movies, there is an underlying moral darkness beneath the surface. Each are genre-bending masterpieces, minus Rat Race and Madhouse. And most importantly, most of the movies come from the mid 90's to late 2010's.
